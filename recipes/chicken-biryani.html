Chicken Biryani

Description

This chicken biryani is a fragrant and flavorful dish with tender marinated chicken and basmati rice cooked together with spices and fresh herbs. Garnished with cilantro, mint, and optional fried onions, it's a hearty, aromatic meal perfect for special occasions or family dinners.

To read more about the history of biryani from wikipedia: Biryani

Preparation Time: 2 hours (minimum) Cook Time: 30 mins Serves: 4-6

Ingredients

  • Chicken Marinade

    • 1 lb chicken, cut into medium-sized pieces
    • 1 cup plain yogurt
    • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
    • 1 tbsp red chili powder
    • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
    • 1 tsp garam masala
    • salt to taste

  • Rice

    • 2 cups basmati rice
    • 4 cups water
    • 2-3 green cardamom pods
    • 4-5 cloves
    • 1 bay leaf
    • salt to taste

  • Biryani

    • 2 tbsp ghee or vegetable oil
    • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
    • 2-3 green chilies , slit lengthwise
    • 1 large tomato, chopped
    • 1/2 cup coriander leaves, chopped
    • 1/2 cup mint leaves, chopped
    • 1 tsp cumin leaves
    • 1 tsp coriander powder
    • 1/2 tsp saffron threads*
    • 1/4 cup warm milk*
    • 1/2 cup fried onions*
    • additional coriander and mint leaves*
* - optional ingredient

Steps

  1. Marinate the Chicken

    Mix the yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, and salt. Add chicken pieces and coat well. Marinate for at least 1 hour or overnight in the fridge.

  2. Prepare the Rice

    Rinse the basmati rice until the water runs clear. Soak it in water for 30 minutes, then drain. In a large pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add cardamom, cloves, bay leaf, and salt. Add the rice and cook until it's 70-80% done (it should still be firm). Drain and set aside.

  3. Cook the Chicken

    Heat ghee or oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle. Add sliced onions and green chilies. Saut√© until onions are golden brown. Add the marinated chicken and cook until the chicken is done and the oil starts to separate from the mixture (about 15-20 minutes). Stir in chopped tomatoes, cilantro, and mint. Cook for another 5 minutes.

  4. Layer the Biryani

    In the same pot, spread a layer of the partially cooked rice over the chicken mixture. If using saffron, dissolve saffron threads in warm milk and drizzle over the rice. You can also add a layer of fried onions if desired. Cover the pot tightly with a lid or aluminum foil.

  5. Dum Cook the Biryani

    Cook on very low heat for 20-30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld and the rice to finish cooking. Remove from heat and let it sit, covered, for an additional 10 minutes.

  6. Serve

    Gently fluff the biryani with a fork before serving. Garnish with additional cilantro and mint.
